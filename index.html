Fundamentals of GIS Final Project

Analysis Overview

This project explores how map projections influence our perception of geographic "truth." Because a 3D sphere cannot be perfectly flattened, every projection inherently distorts area, direction, or distance.

  • Robinson: A natural-looking compromise; curved poles.
  • Goode Homolosine: Interrupted oceans; accurate land proportions.
  • Mercator: Navigational angles preserved; polar size inflation.
  • Bonne: Heart-shaped; distortion increases from center.
Skills Learned
  • Map Projections & CRS: Mastered coordinate systems and mathematical impacts.
  • Defining vs. Projecting: Technical distinction between defining and converting CRS.
  • ArcGIS Pro Proficiency: Advanced layout and layer management.
